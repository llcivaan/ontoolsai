🏠 The Complete Customer Communication Guide for Roofing Business Owners

Communication is one of the biggest challenges in running a roofing business. This guide covers every message you'll ever need to send — from quote follow-ups and review requests to complaint responses and job ads — with copy-paste templates and practical tips for each.

How to Follow Up On A Quote

Most roofing jobs are lost not because the quote was too high, but because no one followed up. A timely, friendly follow-up message can double your conversion rate without any price changes.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], just checking in on the roofing quote I provided on [Date]. Feel free to ask any questions — I'm happy to walk you through the materials or timeline. Call [Phone] or reply here. — [Your Name], [Business Name]

  • Opens with a helpful offer rather than a pushy sales pitch
  • Makes it easy for the customer to reply with a simple yes or no

How to Ask Customers For Google Reviews

Google reviews are the most valuable marketing asset a roofing business has. The best time to ask is immediately after completing a successful job — when the customer's satisfaction is at its peak.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], really glad we could take care of your roof! If you're happy with the work, a Google review helps homeowners find us and helps our business grow. Link here: [Google Review Link]. Thanks! — [Your Name]

  • Sends immediately after the job while the positive experience is fresh
  • Includes a direct link so customers don't have to search for your business

How to Respond To A Bad Google Review

How you respond to a negative review tells potential customers more about your business than the review itself. A calm, professional response can turn a 1-star review into a trust-building asset.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], thank you for the feedback. We're sorry you had a poor experience and want to understand what happened. Please call [Phone] so we can speak directly and find a resolution. — [Business Name]

  • Acknowledges the complaint without admitting fault prematurely
  • Takes the conversation offline which signals maturity to readers

How to Send An Invoice Reminder

Late payments are one of the biggest cash flow challenges for roofing businesses. A clear, friendly reminder sent on the due date recovers most overdue invoices without awkward phone calls.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], a reminder that invoice #[Invoice No.] for [Amount] is outstanding. Please pay via [Payment Method] or contact us at [Phone] with any queries. If already settled, please disregard. — [Business Name]

  • Assumes the best — treats late payment as an oversight, not an intent to avoid
  • Provides clear payment details so there's no excuse not to pay

How to Notify Customers You'Re Running Late

Traffic, overruns, and unexpected delays happen in every roofing business. Proactively texting customers when you're running late can completely prevent negative reviews and appointment frustration.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], quick update — we're running about [X] minutes behind today. Expect us around [New Time]. If that doesn't work for you, call [Phone] and we'll reschedule at no hassle. — [Your Name]

  • Gives the customer maximum time to adjust their schedule
  • Provides a realistic updated arrival window so they know what to expect

How to Handle A Customer Complaint

Customer complaints are an opportunity in disguise. Roofing business owners who respond to complaints quickly and professionally turn unhappy customers into loyal ones more often than you'd think.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], I'm sorry to hear there's an issue with the work. We guarantee our craftsmanship and will come out to inspect and fix anything not up to standard. What day works for you this week? — [Business Name]

  • Leads with empathy before moving to solutions — customers need to feel heard first
  • Offers a concrete next step rather than vague promises

How to Follow Up After A Customer No-Show

Customer no-shows are frustrating and costly. A well-crafted follow-up message recovers most bookings and keeps the relationship intact without confrontation.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], our roofer stopped by today for your appointment but wasn't able to make contact. Happy to rebook at a time that suits you — call [Phone] or reply here. — [Business Name]

  • Gives the customer the benefit of the doubt — no blame, just a check-in
  • Makes rescheduling frictionless with a simple reply or call

How to Write A Job Ad

Hiring good roofing workers is one of the hardest parts of growing a trade business. A clear, honest job ad that leads with pay and benefits attracts more qualified applicants than a vague listing.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

🏠 Hiring: Roofer — [City] Pay: $[X]–$[Y]/hr DOE Hours: Full-time, Mon–Fri Established roofing contractor looking for experienced roofers — shingles, metal, and flat roof experience a plus. Safety training provided. Must have own transport. Call or text [Phone] to apply.

  • Leads with pay and perks — the first thing applicants want to know
  • Gives a clear picture of the role so unqualified candidates self-select out

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a quote follow-up message for my roofing business?

Send a short message two to three days after the quote. Reference the quote date, offer to answer questions, and make it easy for them to say yes. The goal is to stay top of mind without applying pressure. OnToolsAI can generate professional quote follow-up messages for roofing businesses instantly.

What's the best way to ask for Google reviews as a roofing business?

Ask immediately after completing a job while the customer is still happy. Keep your message short, thank them personally, and include a direct link to your Google review page. Customers are far more likely to leave a review when you remove all friction from the process.

How should a roofing business respond to a bad review online?

Stay calm, acknowledge the experience, and offer to resolve the issue offline. Never argue or get defensive — other potential customers are reading your response. A well-handled complaint response can actually increase trust with readers who haven't used you yet.

How do I get customers to pay invoices faster in my roofing business?

Send invoice reminders on the due date rather than waiting a week. Offer multiple payment options and keep the message friendly and brief. Most late payments are genuine oversights — a single prompt text often resolves them the same day.

What should I text a customer when I'm running late for a roofing job?

Keep it short: apologise briefly, give a realistic revised arrival time, and offer to reschedule if needed. The earlier you send this message, the better the customer responds. Proactive communication is the hallmark of professional roofing businesses.

How do I handle a difficult customer complaint in my roofing business?

Lead with empathy, not excuses. Acknowledge that something went wrong from their perspective, offer a concrete next step (such as a return visit), and follow up to confirm they're satisfied. Most customers just want to feel heard before anything else.

How do I write a job ad that attracts skilled roofing workers?

Start with pay and perks — that's the first thing applicants look for. Be specific about hours, requirements, and what you provide. A clear, honest job ad filters out poor fits and attracts serious candidates who are a good match for your business.
