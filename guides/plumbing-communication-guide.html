🔧 The Complete Customer Communication Guide for Plumbing Business Owners

Communication is one of the biggest challenges in running a plumbing business. This guide covers every message you'll ever need to send — from quote follow-ups and review requests to complaint responses and job ads — with copy-paste templates and practical tips for each.

How to Follow Up On A Quote

Most plumbing jobs are lost not because the quote was too high, but because no one followed up. A timely, friendly follow-up message can double your conversion rate without any price changes.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], just checking in on the plumbing quote I sent over on [Date]. Happy to go over the details or tweak anything. Call me at [Phone] or reply here whenever suits. — [Your Name], [Business Name]

  • Opens with a helpful offer rather than a pushy sales pitch
  • Makes it easy for the customer to reply with a simple yes or no

How to Ask Customers For Google Reviews

Google reviews are the most valuable marketing asset a plumbing business has. The best time to ask is immediately after completing a successful job — when the customer's satisfaction is at its peak.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], great working with you today! If everything went smoothly, a quick Google review would really help our small business. Direct link: [Google Review Link]. Thanks so much! — [Your Name]

  • Sends immediately after the job while the positive experience is fresh
  • Includes a direct link so customers don't have to search for your business

How to Respond To A Bad Google Review

How you respond to a negative review tells potential customers more about your business than the review itself. A calm, professional response can turn a 1-star review into a trust-building asset.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], we're sorry to hear about your experience. This isn't the standard we hold ourselves to and we'd like to put it right. Please call [Phone] and we'll arrange a visit at no charge. — [Business Name]

  • Acknowledges the complaint without admitting fault prematurely
  • Takes the conversation offline which signals maturity to readers

How to Send An Invoice Reminder

Late payments are one of the biggest cash flow challenges for plumbing businesses. A clear, friendly reminder sent on the due date recovers most overdue invoices without awkward phone calls.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], friendly reminder — invoice #[Invoice No.] for [Amount] is now overdue. Please pay via [Payment Method] or call [Phone] if you have any questions. If payment has been sent, please disregard. — [Business Name]

  • Assumes the best — treats late payment as an oversight, not an intent to avoid
  • Provides clear payment details so there's no excuse not to pay

How to Notify Customers You'Re Running Late

Traffic, overruns, and unexpected delays happen in every plumbing business. Proactively texting customers when you're running late can completely prevent negative reviews and appointment frustration.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], wanted to let you know we're running about [X] minutes behind. We'll be there around [New Time]. Sorry for the inconvenience — if that time doesn't work, call [Phone] and we'll reschedule. — [Your Name]

  • Gives the customer maximum time to adjust their schedule
  • Provides a realistic updated arrival window so they know what to expect

How to Handle A Customer Complaint

Customer complaints are an opportunity in disguise. Plumbing business owners who respond to complaints quickly and professionally turn unhappy customers into loyal ones more often than you'd think.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], I'm sorry to hear there's been an issue. We stand behind our work and will come back to fix anything that isn't right. Can I arrange a return visit this week? — [Your Name], [Business Name]

  • Leads with empathy before moving to solutions — customers need to feel heard first
  • Offers a concrete next step rather than vague promises

How to Follow Up After A Customer No-Show

Customer no-shows are frustrating and costly. A well-crafted follow-up message recovers most bookings and keeps the relationship intact without confrontation.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], our plumber attended your address today for the scheduled appointment but wasn't able to make contact. We're happy to rebook — just call [Phone] or reply here. — [Business Name]

  • Gives the customer the benefit of the doubt — no blame, just a check-in
  • Makes rescheduling frictionless with a simple reply or call

How to Write A Job Ad

Hiring good plumbing workers is one of the hardest parts of growing a trade business. A clear, honest job ad that leads with pay and benefits attracts more qualified applicants than a vague listing.

💬 Copy-Paste Template

🔧 Hiring: Licensed Plumber — [City] Pay: $[X]–$[Y]/hr Hours: Full-time or part-time available Established plumbing company seeking a licensed plumber for residential and light commercial work. All tools provided. Call or text [Phone] to apply.

  • Leads with pay and perks — the first thing applicants want to know
  • Gives a clear picture of the role so unqualified candidates self-select out

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a quote follow-up message for my plumbing business?

Send a short message two to three days after the quote. Reference the quote date, offer to answer questions, and make it easy for them to say yes. The goal is to stay top of mind without applying pressure. OnToolsAI can generate professional quote follow-up messages for plumbing businesses instantly.

What's the best way to ask for Google reviews as a plumbing business?

Ask immediately after completing a job while the customer is still happy. Keep your message short, thank them personally, and include a direct link to your Google review page. Customers are far more likely to leave a review when you remove all friction from the process.

How should a plumbing business respond to a bad review online?

Stay calm, acknowledge the experience, and offer to resolve the issue offline. Never argue or get defensive — other potential customers are reading your response. A well-handled complaint response can actually increase trust with readers who haven't used you yet.

How do I get customers to pay invoices faster in my plumbing business?

Send invoice reminders on the due date rather than waiting a week. Offer multiple payment options and keep the message friendly and brief. Most late payments are genuine oversights — a single prompt text often resolves them the same day.

What should I text a customer when I'm running late for a plumbing job?

Keep it short: apologise briefly, give a realistic revised arrival time, and offer to reschedule if needed. The earlier you send this message, the better the customer responds. Proactive communication is the hallmark of professional plumbing businesses.

How do I handle a difficult customer complaint in my plumbing business?

Lead with empathy, not excuses. Acknowledge that something went wrong from their perspective, offer a concrete next step (such as a return visit), and follow up to confirm they're satisfied. Most customers just want to feel heard before anything else.

How do I write a job ad that attracts skilled plumbing workers?

Start with pay and perks — that's the first thing applicants look for. Be specific about hours, requirements, and what you provide. A clear, honest job ad filters out poor fits and attracts serious candidates who are a good match for your business.
