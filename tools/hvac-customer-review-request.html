HVAC Google Review Request Templates

The short answer: Ask by text on the same day as the job — while the system is running and the customer's relief is still fresh. Below are copy-paste templates for after a repair, after a new installation, and for the customer who already told you they were happy but never left a review.

After a repair or service call

Emergency repair customers are among the easiest to convert to reviews — the emotion is high, the relief is real, and if you ask while it's still fresh, most of them are genuinely happy to help. Send within 2-4 hours of completing the job.

Standard repair — same day text

Template 1 — Short, honest, direct
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Hope the [system] is running well. Would mean a lot if you had 60 seconds to leave us a Google review — it really helps people in [area] find us. Here's the link: [your Google review link]. Thanks so much. — [Your name]
✓ Send same day · Works for repairs, servicing, tune-ups

Emergency callout — capitalise on the gratitude

Template 2 — Emergency response (no heat / no cool)
Hi [Name], glad we could get that sorted for you today — I know it's stressful when the [system] goes down. If you're happy with the work and had a moment, an honest Google review makes a real difference for our business. Here's the direct link: [link]. No pressure at all. — [Your name]
✓ Best performing template type for HVAC — emotional moment = high conversion

After a new installation

Installation customers are proud of their new system and typically well-disposed toward you — but the window is short. Ask 2-3 days after installation, once they've had time to experience it running properly.

Template 3 — Post-installation (2–3 days later)
Hi [Name], [Your name] here. It's been a couple of days since we installed your new [system] — hope it's running exactly as it should. If you're happy with the job, would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It's a big help for our business. Here's the link: [link]. Really appreciate it. — [Your name]
✓ Wait 48–72 hours so they've actually lived with it

When they told you they were happy but never left a review

This is the most common missed opportunity in HVAC. The customer told you on the day it was great — but never left a review. Here's how to follow up without it feeling awkward.

Template 4 — Verbal praise, no review yet
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. You mentioned on the day you were happy with the [job] — really glad to hear it. If you ever had a moment to put that into a Google review, it genuinely helps other homeowners find us. No pressure at all: [link]. — [Your name]
✓ Send 5–7 days after the job · Light and non-pushy
💡 The follow-up rule: One gentle follow-up after 5-7 days is fine if they haven't left a review. After that, leave it. Chasing reviews beyond two messages can sour an otherwise good customer relationship.

What not to say in a review request

❌ Never say this
"Hi [Name], we'd love it if you could leave us a 5-star Google review! It only takes a second. Click here: [link]"

Asking for a 5-star review specifically violates Google's policies and can get your reviews flagged or removed. It also reads as entitled rather than grateful. Ask for "an honest review" or "a quick review if they have a moment" — the result is the same but the framing is more trustworthy.

When should an HVAC company ask for a Google review?
The same day as the job — within a few hours of completing the work, while the system is running and the customer's satisfaction is fresh. Emergency callout customers in particular are highly motivated to leave reviews immediately after. Don't wait days — the window closes fast.
What should an HVAC review request text say?
Under 3 sentences. Reference the specific job, use their name, be honest that reviews help your business, and include a direct link. Never ask for a 5-star review specifically — that violates Google's policies. Ask for honest feedback or "a quick review if they have a moment."
How many reviews should an HVAC company aim for?
Research consistently shows that 50+ reviews with a rating above 4.5 stars captures the majority of local search clicks. The businesses that get there fastest are the ones who ask after every single job, not just when they remember to.
Should HVAC companies respond to Google reviews?
Yes — always, especially negative ones. A thoughtful response to a bad review demonstrates more competence than 100 five-star ratings. Future customers are reading your responses to see how you handle problems. Businesses that respond well to complaints consistently win more bookings than those that ignore them.
