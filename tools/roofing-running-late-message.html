Roofer Running Late Message Templates

The short answer: Text the customer the moment you know the crew will be delayed — not after you're already late. Roofing customers often need to be home for access or have arranged their day around your arrival. A proactive message protects the relationship and is especially important when weather is involved. Running late message templates

Crew running 30+ minutes late

Template 1 — Crew delay
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Just a heads-up — the team is running about [X] minutes behind this morning. We're now looking at arriving around [new time]. Sorry for the inconvenience — see you shortly. — [Your name]
✓ Send as soon as you know · Customers appreciate the early warning

Weather delay — unsafe conditions

Template 2 — Weather cancellation
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Unfortunately we're going to need to postpone today's start — the [wind / rain / forecast] makes it unsafe to work at height and would affect the quality of the job. I want to do this properly, not rush it in bad conditions. I'll be in touch first thing tomorrow to confirm a new start date. Really sorry for the disruption. — [Your name]
✓ Weather safety is a legitimate and respected reason in roofing · Be specific about the reason

Multi-day job — earlier finish than expected

Template 3 — Earlier than planned
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Good news — we're ahead of schedule and should be wrapping up today rather than [tomorrow / original date]. Just wanted to flag it so you're not surprised when you see us packing up. Everything's looking great. — [Your name]
✓ Finishing early is also worth a message — keeps customers informed and builds trust
💡 Roofing-specific: Weather is a legitimate reason to delay and customers know it. A message that says "I won't work in these conditions because it affects the quality and safety of the job" positions you as a professional who cares about the result — not just getting paid. That message often generates a positive review on its own.

What should a roofer say when running late to a job?
Text as soon as you know the crew will be late. Include your name, the new arrival time, and a brief reason. Roofing customers often need to be home for access — a proactive heads-up is especially important.
How do you handle a roofing delay due to weather?
Contact the customer the evening before or early morning if bad weather is forecast. Explain that working unsafely affects both quality and safety. Offer a specific rescheduled date. Customers respect contractors who prioritise safety over schedule.
