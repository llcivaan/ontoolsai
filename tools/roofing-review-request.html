Roofing businesses live and die by local reputation — and Google reviews are the most visible part of that reputation.

The roofing review window — it's shorter than you think

For most trade jobs, the satisfaction peak is the day the work is done. For roofing, it's slightly different — customers often feel relief and satisfaction on the final day of a multi-day job, but that satisfaction can start to fade fast once they're living with the disruption aftermath (garden covered in debris, scaffolding still being dismantled, small things they noticed).

Ask on the completion day — ideally when you do the final walkthrough, or that same evening. Don't wait until you send the invoice a week later. The emotional distance between completion day and invoice day is significant, and not always in your favour.

What makes a roofing review request work

The key difference between a review request that gets a response and one that gets ignored: the homeowner needs to feel like they're doing something for a person, not completing a process for a company.

Reference the specific job — "the rear extension flat roof" lands better than "our recent work." Acknowledge the investment they made — it builds goodwill and shows you respect what they went through. Keep it short. And include the link — the most willing reviewer in the world won't leave a review if finding your Google listing takes five steps.

Templates for roofing review requests

Template 1 — Full re-roof or major job

SMS — day of completion or that evening
Hi [Name], [Your name] here from [Business] — really glad we got the [re-roof / new flat roof / ridge work] finished today. It was a pleasure working on your home. If you've got a moment and you were happy with the job, a quick Google review would mean a huge amount to us — it's how other homeowners in [area] find us when they need help. Link here: [link]. Takes about 30 seconds. Thanks so much either way. 🏠

Template 2 — Repair job or emergency call-out

SMS — short, relief-focused, honest about why it matters
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business] — glad we got that sorted for you today. If you're happy with the repair, an honest Google review would genuinely help us out — it's how local homeowners find reliable roofers when they need one. Here's the link: [link]. No worries at all if it's not for you — thanks for having us. — [Your name]

Template 3 — For a customer who expressed satisfaction during the job

More personal — mirrors the relationship built on a longer job
Hi [Name], it was genuinely great working on your property over the last few days — your [roof / extension flat / Victorian stacks] is a nice project and I'm really pleased with how it came out. If you'd be happy to share that on Google, it would make a real difference for a small local business like mine. Here's a direct link: [link]. Hope it serves you well for years. — [Your name], [Business]

Template 4 — Follow-up if they didn't respond to the first ask

One follow-up only — about a week after the first ask
Hi [Name], just resending the Google review link in case it got buried — [link]. No pressure at all, just want to make sure it's easy to find if you did want to leave one. Either way, hope the roof is holding up well — don't hesitate to get in touch if anything ever needs attention. — [Your name], [Business]
💡 Ask in person at the final walkthrough — then follow up with the link. A verbal ask at the moment of handover ("I'd really appreciate a Google review if you've been happy with the work") doubles response rates when followed by the SMS link that evening. The in-person ask creates the intention; the link makes it easy to act on.

What good roofing reviewers actually write — and how to prompt it

The most useful roofing reviews mention: the type of work, the team's professionalism, communication throughout the job, tidiness on completion, and whether the customer would use the business again. You can nudge reviewers toward these topics by including a brief prompt in your message: "If you wanted to mention the [type of job] or how the team handled [aspect of the work], that would be especially helpful for people trying to find a reliable roofer."

Don't be too prescriptive — it can feel like coaching rather than asking. But a light steer toward specifics generates more useful reviews than "tell us what you think."

Roofing review questions answered

When is the best time to ask a roofing customer for a review?
On the day of completion, during or immediately after the final walkthrough. That's when satisfaction and relief are at their highest. Waiting until you send the invoice — typically days or weeks later — loses most of your willing reviewers to simple inertia.
How do I get more Google reviews as a roofing company?
Build the ask into your completion process for every job — not just the ones you think went especially well. Satisfied customers need to be asked; they won't typically volunteer a review unless prompted. A completion day SMS with a review link, sent within hours of finishing, is the single highest-impact habit you can build into your workflow.
What if we did a great job but the customer didn't seem that warm?
Still ask — some customers are reserved by nature but would still leave a positive review if prompted. The worst outcome is no response. What you shouldn't do is not ask a satisfied customer because you're guessing at their enthusiasm level.
How many roofing reviews does my business need?
In most local markets, 30-60 reviews with a 4.5+ average gives strong visibility. Because roofing is infrequent for individual homeowners, new reviews from different customers matter — aim for consistency (a few per month) rather than a burst of reviews followed by silence. Google values recency.
