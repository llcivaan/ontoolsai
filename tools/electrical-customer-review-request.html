Review request text templates

Plumbing jobs — especially emergency call-outs — leave customers genuinely relieved and grateful. That window is your highest-converting moment. Ask while they're still thinking about you.

After an emergency or same-day job

Template 1 — Emergency job, same day
Hi [Name], really glad we could get that [job — e.g. power fault / safety switch trip] sorted for you today. If you have a spare 60 seconds, a Google review would mean a lot to us — it genuinely helps other homeowners find us when they need help fast. Here's the link: [your Google review link]. Thanks again — [Your name]
✓ Send within 2-3 hours of completing the job

After a planned job or installation

Template 2 — Planned job, next day
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Hope the [job — e.g. new switchboard / solar system] is all working well. If you're happy with the work, we'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps us a lot and only takes a minute. [your Google review link]. Cheers — [Your name]
✓ Send the day after completion, once they've lived with it

After a check-in reply (two-step approach)

Template 3 — Follow up a positive check-in response
Really glad to hear it! If you ever had 60 seconds to leave us a Google review, it genuinely makes a big difference for our business — here's the link: [your Google review link]. No worries if not, and thanks again. — [Your name]
✓ Use after they reply positively to a post-job check-in
💡 The two-step method: Send a check-in text first ("how's everything since we were out?"). Wait for a positive reply. Then ask for the review. This approach converts at higher rates than asking cold because the customer has just re-engaged with the positive experience.

What not to send

❌ Avoid this
"Please leave us a 5-star review on Google. Thank you for your business."

Asking for a specific star rating violates Google's policies and reads as desperate. Ask for an honest review — a genuine 4-star review from a real customer is more trusted than a suspicious cluster of 5-star reviews with no text.

How should an electrician ask for a Google review?
By text, on the same day as the job, while the customer's relief is still fresh. A short, personal message that references the specific job converts far better than a generic review request email. Include a direct link to your Google review page and keep the ask brief.
When is the best time to ask a plumbing customer for a review?
Within a few hours of completing the job. For emergency plumbing especially — where you showed up when they had no power — the gratitude window is short. Ask the same day. The longer you wait, the lower the conversion rate.
How many Google reviews does a electrical business need?
40+ reviews at 4.5 stars consistently wins the majority of local search clicks. Most homeowners check reviews before calling an electrician — review volume and recency both matter for local ranking.
What if a plumbing customer leaves a bad review?
Respond calmly within 24 hours. Acknowledge the issue, offer to resolve it, and take the conversation offline. A professional response to a bad review often impresses prospective customers more than a perfect rating.
Can an electrician offer a discount in exchange for a review?
No — incentivising reviews violates Google's policies and risks removal or penalties. Ask directly, at the right moment, with a personal message. This converts at a high rate without any policy risk.
