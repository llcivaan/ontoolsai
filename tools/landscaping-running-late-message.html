Running late message templates

Landscaping customers — particularly those with recurring maintenance visits — have often planned their day around your arrival. A quick, proactive message protects the relationship and takes 15 seconds to send.

Running 15–30 minutes late

Template 1 — Short delay
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Just wanted to give you a heads-up — I'm running about [X] minutes behind. Should be with you around [new time]. Sorry for the delay — see you shortly. — [Your name]
✓ Send the moment you know · Don't wait until you're already late

Running more than 30 minutes late

Template 2 — Longer delay, offer to reschedule
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. I'm sorry — I'm running significantly late today. Current ETA is around [new time]. I completely understand if that doesn't work — happy to reschedule to [alternative day/time] if you'd prefer. Just let me know. — [Your name]
✓ Always offer to reschedule for delays over 30 minutes

Weather delay or cancellation

Template 3 — Weather cancellation (unique to landscaping)
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Unfortunately I'm going to need to reschedule today's visit — the [rain / wet ground / forecast] means the [lawn / garden] won't cut / come up well in these conditions. I'll be in touch to book a replacement slot as soon as the weather clears. Sorry for the short notice. — [Your name]
✓ Landscaping-specific — weather cancellations are common and customers understand them
💡 Weather is your wildcard. Landscaping is one of the few trades where a weather cancellation is not only understandable — it's actually professional. Explaining that cutting a wet lawn will damage the grass shows expertise and care. Customers respect it.

What should a landscaper say when running late?
Text the customer as soon as you know you'll be late. Include your name, the new estimated arrival time, and a brief reason. Customers who are informed early are far more forgiving than those who find out by waiting.
Should a landscaper message when running late for a recurring customer?
Yes, every time. Recurring maintenance customers have built their schedule around your visits. A heads-up protects a long-term contract and almost always gets a friendly response.
Does messaging when running late affect landscaping reviews?
Yes — significantly. Customers who receive a proactive running-late message report much higher satisfaction than those kept waiting without notice. It frequently turns a potential negative into a positive mention in reviews.
