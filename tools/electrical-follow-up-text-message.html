Quote follow-up text messages

Electrical quotes — especially for larger jobs like switchboard upgrades, rewiring, or solar installations — often sit quiet while customers compare prices. A follow-up that acknowledges their likely hesitations converts far better than one that just nudges for a decision.

First follow-up — 2 to 3 days after the quote

Template 1 — Warm check-in
Hi [Name], it's [Your name] from [Business]. Just following up on the quote I sent for the [job — e.g. switchboard upgrade / rewire]. If you've got any questions about the work or what's involved, happy to talk you through it. No pressure at all — just want to make sure you've got everything you need to decide. — [Your name]
✓ SMS-ready · Personalise the job type
Template 2 — Addresses cost hesitation directly
Hi [Name], [Your name] here. Quick follow-up on the [job] quote. I know electrical quotes can feel like a lot — if it helps, I'm happy to break down exactly what's included or talk through whether there are any ways to phase the work. No obligation, just want to make sure you're comfortable. — [Your name]
✓ Works well for larger renovation or repiping quotes

Second follow-up — 7 to 10 days

Template 3 — Practical urgency
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Just checking back in on the [job] quote. If the [issue — e.g. leak / hot water] is still causing problems, it's worth getting it sorted before it gets worse — and I've still got your job on file ready to go. Let me know if you'd like to move forward. — [Your name]
✓ Use when the job involves an ongoing issue

Soft close — 2 to 3 weeks

Template 4 — Final message, no pressure
Hi [Name], last follow-up from me on the [job] — I don't want to keep chasing if the timing's not right. The quote is still valid and I'd be happy to revisit it whenever works for you. Hope everything's holding up in the meantime. — [Your name]
✓ Final message — do not follow up after this
💡 For urgent electrical jobs (no power, tripped safety switch, urgent fault): compress the timeline. Follow up within 24 hours — the customer is likely calling multiple plumbers and will book whoever responds fastest and follows up most professionally.

Post-job check-in messages

A quick text after completing a electrical job catches problems early, builds loyalty, and naturally leads into a review request. Most plumbers don't send one — it's a simple differentiator.

Template 5 — Next-day check-in after a repair
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Just checking in — how's the [job — e.g. tap / pipe / hot water] going since we were out? If anything seems off, just let me know and I'll sort it. — [Your name]
✓ Send within 24 hours · Keep it short
Template 6 — After a larger job or installation
Hi [Name], [Your name] here. It's been a couple of days since we finished the [job]. Just wanted to check everything's working the way it should. If you've noticed anything or have any questions, happy to help. — [Your name]
✓ Send 48-72 hours after completion · Sets up review request naturally

What not to send

❌ Never send this
"Hi [Name], just following up on the quote I sent. Let me know if you have any questions. Thanks."

This message adds nothing new, gives the customer no reason to respond, and is ignored by the vast majority of recipients. Every follow-up should contain something the customer didn't already know.

What should a electrical follow-up text message say?
Reference the specific job, use the customer's name, and add something useful. Never just say "checking in on the quote." The best electrical follow-ups acknowledge that the customer is weighing cost versus urgency and offer a clear next step without pressure.
How soon should you follow up after a plumbing quote?
For urgent jobs (active leak, no hot water), follow up within 24-48 hours. For non-urgent work like rewiring or switchboard upgrades, 3-5 days is right. The key is following up before the customer books someone else.
How many follow-up messages should an electrician send?
Two to three. First at 2-3 days, second at 7-10 days, optional soft close at 2-3 weeks. Never follow up more than three times — it damages the relationship and electrical businesses run on referrals.
Should plumbers follow up by text or phone?
Text first for non-emergency follow-ups. For urgent jobs where the customer has a live issue, a direct call is appropriate — the stakes are high enough that it doesn't feel intrusive.
What is a good electrical follow-up text after completing a job?
Short and specific. Reference what was fixed and check it's working. "Hi [Name], just checking in — how's the [job] holding up since we were out? If anything seems off, just reply here." If they respond positively, that's the moment to ask for a Google review.
