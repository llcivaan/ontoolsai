Trade Business Review Request Templates

The short answer: The best trade review requests are specific, personal, and sent on the day of the job. Generic templates work — personalised ones work better. Below are copy-paste review request templates for every trade, plus trade-specific timing advice and a tool that writes a personalised version for your exact customer in 30 seconds.

Universal trade review request template

This template works for any trade. Personalise the job type and your trade name.

Template 1 — Works for any trade (SMS)
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Hope you're happy with the [job/service] today. If you had 60 seconds, an honest Google review makes a huge difference for our business — it helps people in [area] find us. Here's the direct link: [review link]. Really appreciate it. — [Your name]
Template 2 — Slightly warmer version
Hi [Name], it was great [working in your home / on your property / on your system] today. If you're happy with the result, an honest review on Google would mean a lot to us — here's the link: [review link]. No pressure, just genuinely appreciated. — [Your name], [Business]

When is the best time to ask a trade customer for a review?
On the day of the job — within hours of completing the work, while the customer's satisfaction is still fresh. The exact timing varies by trade: cleaning and HVAC work best 1-4 hours after completion; plumbing and electrical the same day; roofing and landscaping 1-2 days after, once the customer has seen the result properly.
What makes a good trade review request message?
Three things: it references the specific job (not just 'our service'), it uses the customer's name, and it includes a direct link to your Google review page. Keep it under 4 sentences. Don't ask for a 5-star review — that violates Google's guidelines. Ask for honest feedback or 'a quick review if they have a moment.'
How many Google reviews does a trade business need?
50+ reviews with a rating above 4.5 stars consistently wins the local search click over competitors. The fastest way to get there is asking after every single job, not occasionally. Businesses that build a review request into their post-job routine typically reach 50 reviews within 6 months.
Is it okay to send a follow-up review request?
One follow-up 5-7 days after the first request is fine if the customer hasn't responded. After two messages, leave it — chasing reviews aggressively can sour an otherwise good customer relationship and is unlikely to convert anyway.
