Why roofing software is different from general FSM platforms

Roofing has specific operational requirements that general field service management platforms handle poorly. Aerial roof measurements that tie directly into estimates, insurance claim workflows for storm restoration work, material ordering from specific roofing suppliers like ABC Supply and SRS Distribution, digital proposals that homeowners can sign on site, photo documentation for insurance supplements — none of these are built into Jobber or Housecall Pro.

For a small roofing business doing primarily repairs and occasional replacements, a general FSM platform like Jobber handles the scheduling and invoicing side adequately. The moment your business model shifts toward residential replacements, storm restoration, or insurance work, a roofing-specific platform pays for itself through faster estimating, better close rates on proposals, and cleaner insurance supplement workflows.

Best operations software for roofing businesses in 2026

JobNimbus
Best all-in-one for residential roofing
From $149/mo (Connect) · $299/mo (Grow) · $449/mo (Manage)
JobNimbus is the most commonly recommended all-in-one platform for residential roofing contractors, particularly those doing insurance restoration work. Its Kanban-style pipeline tracks leads from first contact through signed contract — giving you visibility into where every potential job stands at any moment. Insurance claim workflows are built specifically for how restoration roofing operates. EagleView integration allows aerial roof measurements to flow directly into estimates without re-entry. ABC Supply ordering integration means material orders go out without leaving the platform. Digital proposals let homeowners review and sign on site. The honest caveat: multiple reviewers note that JobNimbus requires real configuration to work well for your specific workflow — plan for two to three weeks of setup before it runs smoothly.
Best for: Residential roofing contractors doing insurance restoration work, managing a sales process from lead to close, and needing aerial measurements integrated into their estimates. The Connect plan works for small operations; Grow and Manage plans for teams managing 20 to 100 jobs per month.
Roofr
Best for affordable aerial measurements and proposals
From $49/mo (Starter) · $99/mo (Essentials) · $169/mo (Pro)
Roofr is the most accessible entry point into roofing-specific software. Its aerial roof measurement reports are delivered in as little as 2 hours and cost $13 per report on the free plan. The built-in proposal tool turns measurement reports into professional homeowner-facing proposals that include material options and digital signatures. On Capterra and G2, roofing contractors specifically describe Roofr as the tool that improved their proposal close rates — because proposals go out faster, look more professional, and give homeowners clear material choices rather than a confusing line-item estimate. The limitation: Roofr is primarily an estimating and proposal tool, not a full operations platform. You will still need separate software for scheduling, CRM, and job management beyond estimating. Works well alongside Jobber for small residential operations.
Best for: Residential roofing contractors who need fast, affordable aerial measurements and professional proposals. Best used alongside a scheduling platform. The accuracy gap vs EagleView matters mainly for complex commercial roofs — for standard residential work, Roofr's accuracy is sufficient.
Jobber
Best for small shops doing repairs without insurance complexity
From $69/mo
Jobber works well for small roofing businesses doing primarily repairs, maintenance, and occasional straightforward replacements — where the main operational needs are clean scheduling, professional invoicing, QuickBooks sync, and a functional mobile app. If aerial measurements, insurance claim workflows, or material ordering with roofing-specific suppliers are not daily requirements, Jobber covers the operational side without the complexity or cost of a roofing-specific platform. The 14-day free trial makes it easy to assess whether it fits before committing. The clear limitation: as soon as storm restoration or high-volume replacement work becomes your core business, Jobber's feature set is insufficient and you will need to migrate to JobNimbus or AccuLynx.
Best for: Small roofing businesses primarily doing repairs and basic replacements without insurance restoration workflows. A practical and affordable starting point before the business needs roofing-specific tools.
AccuLynx
Enterprise roofing — established operations only
~$120/user/mo (per-user pricing)
AccuLynx has the most comprehensive feature set in dedicated roofing software — deep insurance supplement workflows, detailed production management, strong reporting, and an established reputation particularly among storm restoration companies. The challenge is cost. At approximately $120 per user per month, a crew of 10 on the Premium plan runs $1,200 per month — $14,400 per year before setup costs. Per-user pricing punishes growth: doubling from 10 to 20 crew members doubles the monthly bill. AccuLynx is the right tool for roofing companies doing $1 million or more in annual revenue with dedicated back-office staff and the volume to justify the investment. For smaller operations, JobNimbus delivers comparable workflow management at a more predictable cost.
Best for: Established roofing companies doing $1M or more annually, doing high-volume insurance restoration work, with dedicated staff to manage the platform and the revenue to absorb per-user pricing at scale.
ServiceTitan
Enterprise — large roofing operations only
$245–$500/crew member/mo
ServiceTitan has invested significantly in roofing, including becoming the Preferred CRM of GAF and integrating EagleView and Hover for aerial measurements. For large roofing enterprises with 20 or more crews, the AI dispatch, marketing automation, and supplier integrations are genuinely powerful. The cost, 12-month contract requirement, and 6 to 12 month implementation timeline make it the wrong choice for any roofing business under $2 million annual revenue. JobNimbus and AccuLynx deliver roofing-specific depth at a fraction of the cost for most operations.
Best for: Large roofing enterprises with 20 or more crews, dedicated back-office and marketing staff, and the revenue to justify the cost and 12-month contract. Not suitable for businesses below $2M annual revenue.

The cost trap to watch for

Per-user pricing in roofing software compounds quickly as your business grows. AccuLynx at $120 per user per month costs $1,200 per month for 10 people and $2,400 per month for 20 people. JobNimbus's flat-rate plans ($149, $299, $449) are more predictable as you scale. Before committing to any per-user platform, calculate what the monthly cost looks like at your current team size and at double that — the number that matters is where you are in 2 years, not today.

The communication gap roofing software leaves open

Roofing is one of the highest-value decisions a homeowner makes. A roof replacement costing $10,000 to $20,000 involves real deliberation — multiple quotes, sometimes insurance involvement, and significant financial stress. The businesses that convert the highest proportion of replacement quotes are the ones whose follow-up communication builds trust rather than just chasing a decision.

Every roofing platform on this list handles the operational side of quoting — aerial measurements, material selection, proposal generation. What none of them does is write the Day 4 follow-up message that gives a hesitant homeowner a specific reason to respond. The message that says "Happy to walk through any part of the quote — a lot of homeowners want to understand exactly what materials we're using, or want to see photos from similar jobs before they decide." This message, sent consistently on every unanswered quote, converts a meaningful proportion of jobs that would otherwise be assumed lost to a competitor.

The review request is the other gap. On the day of completion — within 2 hours of the crew finishing — the homeowner is standing outside looking at their new roof for the first time. The relief that the disruption is over, the pride in the visible improvement, the satisfaction of a significant investment completed: this is the peak emotional moment for a review. JobNimbus can send an automated notification. What it cannot send is a personal message from the business owner referencing the specific job and encouraging the homeowner to share photos. That personal, specific, timely request converts at a rate that automated platform reminders do not approach.
