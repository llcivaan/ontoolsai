The key difference — automation versus personalisation

NiceJob is built on one core insight: most trade businesses never ask for reviews, so automating the ask produces dramatically more reviews than doing nothing. That insight is correct, and NiceJob executes it well. The platform connects to your CRM, detects when jobs close, and fires a review request automatically — no manual action required.

OnToolsAI takes a different approach. Instead of automating a generic campaign, it writes a personalised message you send yourself — one that references the specific job, uses the customer's name, and sounds like you wrote it. The trade-off is manual effort versus conversion quality.

The review timing insight most businesses miss

The single most important factor in getting Google reviews is timing — not the tool you use. A review request sent within 2 hours of completing a job converts at a dramatically higher rate than one sent the following day. The customer's relief and satisfaction is at its peak immediately after the work is done. By the next morning, they have moved on.

OnToolsAI writes a personalised review request in under 30 seconds, specifically mentioning what was done at that job. NiceJob sends an automated trigger when the job is marked as closed in your CRM. Both approaches can hit the right timing window. The difference is that one message sounds like it came from you — and for a local trade business where trust is the product, that personalisation converts better at lower volume.
