The Situation

You need to hire a technician, helper, or crew member. A well-written job ad attracts qualified applicants and filters out poor fits.

Copy-Paste Template

❄️ Hiring: HVAC Technician — [City] Pay: $[X]–$[Y]/hr DOE Hours: Full-time, Mon–Fri Looking for a certified HVAC tech with 2+ years experience. EPA 608 required. Company van, tools, and uniform provided. Text your resume to [Phone] or email [Email].

Why This Message Works

  • Leads with pay and perks — the first thing applicants want to know
  • Gives a clear picture of the role so unqualified candidates self-select out
  • Ends with a simple, low-friction application step to maximise responses

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a job ad that attracts good hvac workers?

Lead with pay, hours, and perks — that's what applicants check first. Be honest about the role and what's required. OnToolsAI helps hvac business owners write clear, effective job ads that attract quality applicants.

Where should I post a job ad for hvac workers?

Facebook Jobs, Indeed, and local Facebook groups are the highest-ROI platforms for trade workers. Nextdoor and community notice boards also work well for local hires. Don't overlook trade school job boards.

Should I list a salary range in my hvac job ad?

Yes — job ads with pay ranges get significantly more applications. Candidates appreciate transparency and it saves both parties time if the compensation isn't a fit. Listing a range is better than listing nothing.

What should I include in a hvac job ad?

Pay rate, hours and schedule, required qualifications or licences, what you provide (tools, vehicle, uniform), and a simple application step. Keep it scannable — most people read job ads on their phones.
