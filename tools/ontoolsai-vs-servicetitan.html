The honest truth about ServiceTitan for small trade businesses

ServiceTitan is not a bad product. For a 30-technician HVAC company dispatching 100 residential calls a day, it is genuinely among the most powerful platforms available. The problem is that thousands of small trade business owners — plumbers with 2 vans, electricians with 4 team members, cleaning operators with 6 staff — are sold ServiceTitan as a growth tool, only to discover they are paying enterprise prices for complexity they cannot use.

ServiceTitan itself has acknowledged this in BBB responses, stating the platform is not optimised for companies with 3 or fewer technicians and works best for businesses with mature operational processes. That is honest — but it is buried in small print far from the sales page.

⚠️ The real cost of ServiceTitan for a small trade business

A 5-technician HVAC or plumbing company on ServiceTitan's Essentials plan pays approximately $1,500 to $2,500 per month in subscription costs alone — before a $5,000 to $15,000 one-time implementation fee, before the 6 to 12 month onboarding period during which they are paying full price before the platform is even operational, and before add-on costs for Marketing Pro, Phones Pro, and Pricebook Pro. Multiple contractor forums report being quoted $39,375 buyout fees when trying to exit a 2-year contract 10 days after starting.

Who ServiceTitan is actually built for

ServiceTitan makes the most sense for a specific type of business: a residential service company dispatching 20 or more technicians daily, with a dedicated office manager or dispatcher, a marketing budget for campaigns, and a 6 to 12 month runway to implement a new platform without losing operational momentum. At that scale, the reporting depth, payroll integration, and call centre tools genuinely pay for themselves.

If that describes your business, ServiceTitan is worth evaluating — with eyes open about the contract terms and implementation timeline.

If you are a plumber with 3 vans, an HVAC company with 8 technicians, or a cleaning business with 10 staff, the mismatch between what ServiceTitan is built for and what you actually need is significant. Multiple independent reviews from 2026 describe the experience as paying enterprise prices for complexity that overwhelms a small team, with an implementation period that costs more in disruption than the platform saves in its first year.
