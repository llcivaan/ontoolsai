Running late message templates

In plumbing, customers sometimes have a live issue — a leak, no hot water, a blocked drain. Keeping them waiting without a message is one of the fastest ways to damage trust before you've even arrived. A quick, honest text fixes that.

Running 15–30 minutes late

Template 1 — Short delay
Hi [Name], it's [Your name] from [Business]. Just wanted to give you a heads-up — I'm running about [X] minutes behind schedule. Should be with you around [new time]. Sorry for any inconvenience — see you shortly. — [Your name]
✓ Send as soon as you know · Don't wait until you're already late

Running more than 30 minutes late

Template 2 — Longer delay, offer to reschedule
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. I'm really sorry — I'm running significantly behind today due to [brief reason — e.g. a job that's taken longer than expected]. I'm now looking at arriving around [new time]. I completely understand if that doesn't work for you — happy to reschedule if you'd prefer. Just let me know. — [Your name]
✓ Always offer to reschedule for delays over 30 minutes

On the way — arrival heads-up

Template 3 — On my way message (standard)
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business] — I'm on my way and should be with you in around [X] minutes. See you shortly. — [Your name]
✓ Send 15-20 minutes before arrival · Customers appreciate the heads-up
💡 For emergency plumbing jobs especially: the customer called you stressed. A proactive running-late message — even for a short delay — signals professionalism and dramatically reduces the chance of a complaint or a bad review, even if you arrive late.

What should a plumber say when running late to a job?
Text the customer as soon as you know you'll be late. Include your name, the approximate new arrival time, and a brief reason. Customers who are informed early are far more forgiving than those who find out by waiting.
How early should a plumber message when running late?
As soon as you know. Giving a customer 30 minutes notice is far better than arriving 30 minutes late with no warning. Messages sent at least 20-30 minutes before the original arrival time are considered acceptable by most customers.
Does messaging when running late affect reviews?
Yes — significantly. Customers who receive a proactive delay message report much higher satisfaction than those who wait without notice, even if the total wait time is identical. A prompt running-late message often turns a potential complaint into a non-issue.
