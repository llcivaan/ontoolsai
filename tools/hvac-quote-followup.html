❄️ How to Follow Up On A Quote as a HVAC Business Owner

For hvac business owners, knowing how to follow up on quotes professionally can make the difference between a lost customer and a loyal one. This guide gives you a proven, copy-paste-ready template and explains exactly why it works — with specific advice for hvac businesses.

Why This Matters for HVAC Businesses

Most HVAC quotes go quiet not because the customer chose someone cheaper — but because nobody followed up. Air conditioning and heating jobs aren't impulse decisions. The customer is comparing you against one or two others, weighing up cost, trust and timing. If you don't follow up, you're essentially stepping aside and letting someone else take the job.

A single well-timed follow-up message can recover 20–30% of quotes that would otherwise go cold. For an HVAC business where a single install can be worth $8,000 or more, that's not a communication nicety — it's a revenue lever. The message above is short enough not to feel pushy, and specific enough to feel human.

The Situation

You sent a quote a few days ago and haven't heard back. The customer seemed interested but went quiet.

Copy-Paste Template

Hi [Name], just following up on the HVAC quote I sent through on [Date]. Happy to walkyou through the options or tweak the scope if the budget needs adjusting. Give me a call on [Phone] or reply here — whatever's easier. — [Your Name], [Business Name]

Why This Message Works

  • It's short and respectful — a 3-line message says 'I'm professional' without coming across as needy
  • It offers help, not pressure — asking if they have questions removes the 'are you buying yet?' vibe completely
  • It removes friction — giving them your phone number makes it effortless to say yes
  • It re-anchors your quote in their mind — many customers forget the details within days, and this puts you front and centre again
  • It sounds like a real person wrote it — not a template, not a bot, just someone who cares about their work

Common Mistakes HVAC Businesses Make

❌ Mistake 1
Waiting too long
Most trades wait a week or more. By then the customer has forgotten you, or worse, booked someone else. Two to three days is the sweet spot.
❌ Mistake 2
Asking 'Did you get my quote?'
This puts the customer on the spot and feels passive-aggressive. Instead, ask if they have any questions about the scope or pricing.
❌ Mistake 3
Sending a long follow-up email
A follow-up should be short — 3 sentences max. Long messages read like you're begging for the job.

When to Send This Message

Send your first follow-up 2–3 business days after the quote. If you sent it on a Friday, wait until Tuesday morning — weekend chasing feels pushy. If they still haven't replied after 5 days, one more short message is fine. After that, move on.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I follow up on a quote without sounding pushy as a HVAC business?

Frame it as a helpful check-in, not a chase. Ask if they have any questions about the quote rather than asking if they're ready to book. Most customers go quiet because they're comparing options, not because they've said no — a calm, professional follow-up often tips the balance.

How long should I wait before following up on a hvac quote?

Two to three business days is the sweet spot. Long enough that they've had time to think, short enough that your quote is still fresh. If you sent it on a Friday, follow up on Tuesday morning.

What should I say when following up on a hvac quote?

Keep it short — reference the quote, offer to answer questions, and make it easy for them to reply. Don't sell hard on the first follow-up. A three-sentence message almost always outperforms a long pitch.

Should I follow up by text or phone call for a hvac quote?

Text or email for the first follow-up — it's low pressure and they can reply in their own time. If you still haven't heard back after another 4–5 days, a phone call is appropriate.

How many times should I follow up on a hvac quote?

Two follow-ups is usually enough. After that, you risk being annoying. Send the first at 2–3 days, the second at about a week. If you still haven't heard back, move your energy to other leads.

What if a customer says they're still thinking about the hvac quote?

That's actually a good sign — they're still in the game. Reply with 'No problem, happy to answer any questions that come up' and leave it there. Don't push for a timeline.
