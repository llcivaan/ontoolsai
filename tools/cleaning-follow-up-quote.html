Cleaning Business Quote Follow-Up Templates

The short answer: Most cleaning quotes go quiet because the customer got busy — not because they found someone else. One well-timed follow-up at 3-5 days, with something useful added, converts a significant portion of those quiet quotes. Below are templates for residential clients, commercial contracts, and the customer who said they'd 'think about it.'

By OnToolsAI · Updated March 24, 2026 · 8 min read

Residential quote follow-up

Residential cleaning quotes are usually decided within a week. One follow-up at 3-5 days, personalised to something from your initial visit, is usually enough.

Template 1 — Friendly 3-day follow-up
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Just following up on the quote I sent over for [cleaning service] — wanted to make sure you had everything you needed to make a decision. Happy to answer any questions or adjust anything if something doesn't quite fit. Looking forward to hopefully working with you. — [Your name]
Template 2 — Follow-up with availability offer
Hi [Name], [Your name] here. Just checking in on the cleaning quote — I have a spot available [this week / week of X] that would suit your place really well if you'd like to get started. No pressure at all — just let me know if you have any questions. — [Your name]

Commercial cleaning quote follow-up

Commercial quotes involve more decision-makers and a longer timeline. Two follow-ups over two weeks are appropriate.

Template 3 — Commercial first follow-up (5-7 days)
Hi [Name], [Your name] from [Business]. Following up on the commercial cleaning proposal I sent over — wanted to check if you or the team had any questions about the scope, frequency, or the products we use. Happy to arrange a quick call or visit if it would help. — [Your name]
Template 4 — Commercial second follow-up (12-14 days)
Hi [Name], last follow-up on the cleaning proposal — just wanted to make sure it hadn't fallen through the cracks. If the timing's not right at the moment, I completely understand. Whenever you're ready to revisit it, the quote is still valid. — [Your name], [Business]

How do you follow up on a cleaning quote without being pushy?
Reference something specific from the initial visit or the quote, add something genuinely useful (availability, a question about their schedule), and frame it as a check-in rather than a chase. 'Wanted to make sure you had everything you needed' reads very differently to 'just following up on the quote I sent.'
How long should you wait before following up on a cleaning quote?
3-5 days for residential clients — they typically decide quickly and a follow-up after 3 days catches them at a natural decision point. For commercial cleaning contracts, 5-7 days is more appropriate — the decision cycle is longer and involves more people.
What if a cleaning client says the price is too high?
Ask what they were expecting before adjusting anything. Often the gap is smaller than it seems, or they're comparing to a different scope. Once you understand the concern, you can either justify the price (frequency, products, thoroughness) or suggest a modified scope that works for their budget — for example, bi-weekly rather than weekly, or main rooms only.
How many times should you follow up on a cleaning quote?
Once for most residential quotes — cleaning clients decide quickly and two follow-ups in a short window feels pressure-heavy. For commercial contracts, two follow-ups over two weeks is reasonable given the longer decision timeline.
